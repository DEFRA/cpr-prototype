Packaging categories

As part of the EPR regulations, you’ll need to:

  • tell us which packaging activities you carried out
  • submit packaging data based on your packaging activities

So that we can let you know what data to prepare, we’ll ask you about your activities for each packaging category.

Packaging definitions and examples

Primary packaging

Primary packaging is what goods are contained in when sold to the end consumer. It’s sometimes called a ‘sales unit’.

 

Example scenario:
Peas are placed into steel tins that have paper labels. The peas are sold to end consumers in these tins.

Primary packaging used:
steel tin and paper label.

Secondary packaging

Secondary packaging is used to group a number of ‘sales units’. Organisations sometimes use it to display goods in shops or sell it to the end consumer along with the goods it contains.

 

Example scenario:
Multiple tins of peas are/the primary packaging is placed into cardboard boxes. These cardboard boxes are sold to retail organisations.

Secondary packaging used:
cardboard box.

Transit packaging

Transit packaging is what sales units or goods grouped in secondary packaging are contained in while they’re being transported or handled. It’s used to prevent damage to the goods.

Transit packaging does not include road, rail, ship and air containers.

 

Example scenario:
The secondary packaging is placed into larger cardboard boxes that are sealed with parcel tape. These boxes are then put onto wooden pallets and covered in shrink wrap before they're transported.

Transit packaging used:
cardboard box, parcel tape, wooden pallet and shrink wrap.
