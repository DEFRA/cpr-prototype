Introduction to the packaging categories

As part of the Extended Producer Responsibility scheme, organisations must submit data about activities they have undertaken within each of the packaging categories.

In the following sections you'll be asked a series of questions about your activities, these will help you to understand how your organisation is obligated, and what data you need to report.

Packaging definitions

Primary packaging

Primary packaging is packaging conceived as a sales unit to the final user or consumer.

Secondary packaging

Secondary packaging is packaging conceived to group a certain number of sales units whether sold to the final user or consumer or a means to stock shelves at the point of sale. It can be removed from the product without affecting its characteristics.

Transit packaging

Transit packaging is packaging conceived to aid handling and transport of a number of sales units or grouped packs in order to prevent physical handling and transport damage. Transit packaging does not include road, rail, ship and air containers.
