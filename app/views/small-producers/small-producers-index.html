Identify/declare/select the ‘class’ of producer

Response to requirement

Response to missing requirement that applicants for producer registration will identify/declare/select the ‘class’ of producer to which they belong as part of that registration journey.

    The possible ‘classes’ are set out in Reg 15 and defined in Regulations 16 to 22:
  • (i) a brand owner (Reg 16);
  • (ii) a packer/filler (Reg 17);
  • (iii) an importer or first UK owner (Reg 18);
  • (iv) a distributor (Reg 19);
  • (v) an online marketplace operator (Reg 20);
  • (vi) a service provider (Reg 21); or
  • (vii) a seller (Reg 22).

Applicants for registration as a producer must specify as part of their application (Sch 3 paras 7 and 8):

  1. Which of the functions described in regulations 16 to 22 the producer performs in relation to packaging.
  2. If the producer performs more than one such function, which function constitutes the producer’s main activity as a producer.

This applies to every producer, however the application for registration is made - e.g. by the producer organisation directly, by a holding company for itself and its subsidiaries, or via a compliance scheme.

And that ‘holding companies' must also register themselves in addition to their subsidiaries (Reg 2 ““group registration” means a registration of a holding company, and one or more subsidiaries which are producers, with the appropriate agency…”. This applies even if the holding company itself wouldn’t otherwise be a ‘producer’ (Sch 9 para 4(1) “An application to the appropriate agency for a group registration must be made by the holding company of a corporate group, whether or not the holding company is a producer.”
